# Cairn Product Answers (Haktan, 2026-07-04)

> Canon notes from a direct product Q&A with Haktan. These answers are durable
> intent and bind content authoring alongside `docs/PAYLOAD_ECONOMY_v0.md` and
> `docs/CONTENT_FACTORY_CONTRACT.md`. Docs-only; no runtime change authorized.

## 1. The learner

Nerdy hobbyists who want to learn the language INSIDE-OUT — people like
Haktan himself. Not tourists on a deadline, not casual streak-chasers. They
want the logic and mechanics, not rote memorization. Influence: the
LearnCraft Spanish podcast (explain the why; drill the real thing).

Authoring consequences:
- Insight cards may go one notch deeper on "why this works" (still no
  conjugation tables, no grammar dumps).
- Transaction-urgency items (c'est combien, l'addition) drop in backlog
  priority; mechanics doorways (pronouns, mood logic) rise.
- The "after-class" scene world used in L7-L15 fits this learner (someone
  studying seriously) and stays.

## 2. Weave, the true form (CONTENT CORRECTION)

Weave must push toward the unknown: French skeleton from owned engines,
unknown parts stay ENGLISH. Example register (Haktan's own):
`je voudrais you to répéter this` · L3-band example: `je voudrais but pas today`.

Rules:
- Every teaching lesson from the next batch onward should carry at least one
  TRUE mixed-language Weave (open weave), alongside the existing
  pure-French supported weaves.
- **W1 (locked):** open mixed Weaves have NO right/wrong grading. The learner
  answers; we run a Natural Reveal; they compare and get exposed to what they
  didn't know. Mechanically: model-answer-only / passive mirror, never
  exact-match validation.
- The L0-L15 pure-French weaves already shipped are fine as the "supported
  weave" tier; the open mixed tier is additive, not a rewrite.

## 3. Natural Reveal placement (locked)

Natural Reveal accompanies ALL free-writing/production exercises (say-it +
open mixed weaves). Chip/option exercises do not need it (the chips already
carry the natural forms).

**W2 (locked): reveal look-ahead window.** The natural form shown in a reveal
may sit ~3-4 lessons ahead of the learner (5-6 at most), recognition-only.
Never far-future forms (no subjunctive at L3). Example: at L3, revealing
around `je voudrais but pas today` is right-sized; `Je voudrais que vous
répétiez ça` is not.

## 4. Audio plan

Hardcoded human recordings will replace TTS (recorded, not generated).
Shadowing (listen-and-repeat, ungraded) is the first speaking feature and
will exist from early on; graded pronunciation comes much later.
Authoring consequence: the rising-intonation pedagogy is safe (learners will
hear real contours); lessons may carry a designNote marking natural shadowing
beats (meet-cards), with NO schema change now. (S1)

## 5. Campfire Mode @ L24 (paywall boundary)

At L24 the paywall enters. A non-paying learner enters Campfire Mode: a
practice loop in the Practice Hub built from THEIR owned words and error
tracking. **C1 (locked): Campfire content is GENERATED, not hardcoded** — the
engine keeps producing fresh recombinations from the learner's inventory; for
retention it may sprinkle words/chips from lessons ahead as teasers.

## 6. The free-tier promise

By the paywall, the free learner must be able to say: "I talk to myself, I
understand, I can build my own sentences." The taste of the learning
threshold should linger. **C2 (accepted, scoped easy):** occasional chip-less
open Say It moments from ~L10 onward — deliberately EASY asks, so the
"I did it without help" feeling lands without frustration.

## 7. First testers

Family and close friends. Polish accordingly: L1-L5 copy should be clean, but
this wave tolerates rough edges that smoke feedback will catch.
