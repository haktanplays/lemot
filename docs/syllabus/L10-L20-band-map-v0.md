## 1. Executive Summary

- **Recommended arc:** **Option C** (below) — a rhythm-respecting, commercial-depth-preserving sequence: **L10 Integration (shipped) → L11 Pouvoir-light → L12 Question-expansion-1 → L13 Integration → L14 `y`-light → L15 Devoir/Falloir-light → L16 Integration + A-Small-Moment seed → L17 Human-context/feelings → L18 Futur-proche stronger-preview (not owned) → L19 Integration/weak-point repair → L20 Pre-Campfire checkpoint (milestone).**
- **Main pedagogical goal of the band:** turn the L1–L10 *survival + movement + small-action* learner into someone who can **ask for and offer help, ask richer questions, express obligation/ability, and handle a short real exchange** — while the **headline production engines stay previewed, not owned**.
- **Main commercial/paywall implication:** Campfire ~L24 is the gate (**settled** — legacy L14 is archive), so **L11–L20 are all free**. The band gives **real, usable ability** (help/permission, richer questions, obligation, feelings, `y`, the A Small Moment ritual) **without owning** the four headline engines (full futur proche, passé composé, full question formation, broad pouvoir + en/partitives) — those are the **reasons to cross Campfire**. The design goal is to **balance usefulness, trust, and preview value while reserving deeper ownership for the paid/promise zone** (§5). A strong free path does **not** require owning a headline engine.
- **Biggest risk:** **the futur-proche ownership point and the resulting free-tier *tuning*** (§5) — i.e. *how much* the pre-Campfire band should do, and how strong the L18 futur preview should be, without spending the headline engines. (The **paywall position itself is settled** — Campfire ~L24; legacy L14 is archive, so it is **not** a product-direction risk here, only a later docs-alignment cleanup.) Secondary risk: **stacking control layers** (pouvoir + question formation + y + devoir) too densely, re-creating the L7–L9 acceleration the Integration Rhythm Rule was written to prevent.

## 4. Candidate L10–L20 Arcs (≥3 options evaluated)

### Option A — Safer / rhythm-first (as proposed in the brief)
L10 Integration ✅ · L11 Pouvoir-light · **L12 Integration/Can-do+Help** · L13 Question-exp-1 · L14 `y` · L15 Review/ASM-seed · L16 Devoir/Falloir · L17 Human-context · L18 Futur-preview · L19 Integration · L20 Gate.
- **Strengths:** maximally safe pacing; every new engine quickly consolidated; near-zero overload risk.
- **Risks:** **over-integration** — L10 + L12 + L15 + L19 + L20 = **5 of 11 lessons are integration/review/gate**, with only *one* new engine (pouvoir) between L10 and L12. High "**too little novelty**" risk; the free band may feel slow/repetitive.
- **Commercial impact:** safe (nothing leaked), but a slow free band can *hurt* conversion (learner stalls before seeing enough value to want Campfire).
- **Pacing impact:** under the rhythm rule's ceiling, but arguably *below the floor* — integration is meant to follow ~3 new engines, not interleave after every one.
- **Recommendation:** **second choice.** Good guardrails, but too cautious; merge its caution into C.

### Option B — Momentum / engine-heavy (as proposed)
L10 Integration ✅ · L11 Pouvoir-light · **L12 Question-exp · L13 `y` · L14 Faire-continuation** · L15 Review · L16 Devoir/Falloir · **L17 En/quantity** · L18 Futur-deeper-preview · L19 ASM · L20 Gate.
- **Strengths:** strong forward momentum; lots of new capability fast.
- **Risks:** **violates the Integration Rhythm Rule** — **L11–L14 is four consecutive new-engine/control lessons** (pouvoir → question → y → faire) before any integration at L15. Re-creates exactly the L7–L9 acceleration the rule was written to stop. Also **L17 En/quantity + partitive** is advanced and **leaks paid-zone value**; **L18 "deeper" futur preview** risks tipping into ownership.
- **Commercial impact:** **negative** — opens too many systems pre-Campfire (en/partitive, deep futur), eroding the promise zone.
- **Pacing impact:** **fails the rule.** Overload-prone.
- **Recommendation:** **reject as-is.** Only viable if heavily re-cut (insert integration after L12, drop en/partitive, keep futur a light preview) — at which point it becomes C.

### Option C — Commercial-depth preserving + rhythm-respecting (RECOMMENDED)
L10 Integration ✅ · L11 Pouvoir-light · L12 Question-exp-1 · **L13 Integration** · L14 `y`-light · L15 Devoir/Falloir-light · **L16 Integration+ASM seed** · L17 Human-context/feelings · L18 Futur stronger-preview · **L19 Integration/repair** · L20 Pre-Campfire checkpoint.
- **Strengths:** **2 new engines → 1 integration** cadence (L11–L12→L13; L14–L15→L16; L17–L18→L19) — healthier than A's over-integration and safe vs B's overload. Headline engines all previewed, none owned. Introduces the **A Small Moment** ritual at L16 as the integration vehicle (novelty without a new grammar system). Ends on a capability milestone (L20) that on-ramps Campfire.
- **Risks:** **L12 (question expansion) and L15 (devoir/falloir) are the two genuine overload candidates** (§7); the **L18 futur preview must be policed hard** to not become ownership.
- **Commercial impact:** **best** — strong free usefulness (help/permission, richer questions, obligation, feelings, `y`) while the four headline engines stay for Campfire+.
- **Pacing impact:** respects the rule with margin; never >2 consecutive new before an integration beat.
- **Recommendation:** **adopt as the working arc** — but treat L12+ as provisional pending §10 gates (especially the futur-proche ownership point + free-tier tuning; the paywall position itself is settled).

## 5. Paywall / Commercial Depth Review

> **Canon (settled): Campfire ~L24 = paywall/promise gate ⇒ L10–L20 are all pre-Campfire / free.** Legacy L14 is archive (see header) — **not** an active competing boundary. The review below protects high-value systems before L24 on that basis.

| System | Classification | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **pouvoir** | **early-free owned (light slice)** at L11 | help/permission is high-utility and socially essential; own `je peux`/`on peut`/`vous pouvez m'aider`. **Broad pouvoir** (probability, polite softening of every request, `il se peut`) → **paid-zone deepened**. |
| **devoir / falloir** | **early-free owned (light slice)** at L15 | `je dois + inf.` / `il faut + inf.` are core A1; light ownership is fine. `il faut que + subjonctif` → **post-paywall**. |
| **futur proche** | **early-free PREVIEW only** (L7 hook → L18 stronger preview); **paid-zone owned** | the "going to *do* X" engine is the single strongest free-tier temptation; previewing builds desire, **owning it pre-Campfire spends the promise**. Own at/after Campfire approach. |
| **full question formation** | **early-free: controlled slice only** (L8 `où` end-placement; L12 `est-ce que` + 1–2 Q-words); **paid-zone: full system** (inversion, all Q-words, embedded questions) | controlled questions are needed to function; the *full* system is deep and reservable. |
| **`y`** | **early-free owned (place sense only)** at L14 | narrow, high-frequency, pairs with movement; other `y` senses + `en` deferred. |
| **`en`** | **paid-zone owned** (NOT in L10–L20 under Option C) | quantity/partitive pronoun is advanced; pairing it with partitives pre-Campfire over-loads and leaks value. |
| **partitives (du/de la/des)** | **early-free: recognition only**; **paid-zone owned** | food/quantity system; keep `pas de` (supported, L5) but defer the partitive *system*. |
| **past tense / passé composé** | **paid-zone owned** (post-Campfire) | the second headline engine; do not open in L10–L20 at all (not even strong preview before L20). |
| **A Small Moment** | **early-free owned (seeded L16, recurs)** | the signature reading-response ritual is a *retention/usefulness* feature, not a grammar engine — safe and motivating to give free; **deeper AI-driven A Small Moment** → paid-zone deepened. |
| **Practice Pool Challenge** | **early-free: Build+Stretch throughout; Challenge from ~L13** | Challenge (free production) should expand as owned engines accumulate; full open-ended Challenge → paid-zone deepened. |
| **Mon Lexique depth** | **early-free: learner notebook (planning only; dev-apk out of scope)**; **paid-zone: deeper AI/Word-Graph** | per DEV_APK canon + engine §14 tiering. |
| **AI Natural Reveal depth** | **early-free: model-answer-only (L0–L20)**; **paid-zone: live AI evaluation** | L0–L10 are all `model-answer-only`; keep AI evaluation for the paid tier (AI contract §3 `feedback_mode`). |

**Objective verdict (both directions):**
- **Giving away too much?** **No, provided the four headline engines (full futur proche, passé composé, full question formation, en+partitives) stay reserved.** Option C does this; **Option B gives away too much** (en/partitive, deep futur).
- **Withholding too much / free path weak?** **No — the free band is strong on its own terms.** Help/permission (pouvoir-light, scoped narrowly as help/permission rather than full pouvoir), richer questions, obligation (devoir-light), feelings, `y`, and the A Small Moment ritual give **real, usable conversational ability** — usefulness + trust + preview value — **without** needing to own a headline engine. The genuine question is a **tuning** one (not a weakness): **how strong should the L18 futur-proche preview be** — light recognition vs near-ownership "supported"? A near-ownership preview gives the free tier one big-feeling capability just before Campfire while **still reserving full ownership for the paid/promise zone**. Recommend leaning on **preview value + A Small Moment + feelings** for perceived strength rather than spending a headline engine early. (Moving the paywall is **not** a lever here — Campfire ~L24 is settled.)

## 6. Pacing Review (Integration Rhythm Rule: ≤ ~3 consecutive new-engine/control lessons)

**Integration/review beats in Option C:** L10 ✅, **L13**, **L16**, **L19**, L20 (milestone). New-engine/control runs between beats: L11–L12 (2), L14–L15 (2), L17–L18 (2). **Never exceeds 3.** ✅

Explicit flags:
- **Too many architecture verbs in a row?** L11 pouvoir + L15 devoir/falloir are the only two architecture verbs in the band, **well separated** (L13 integration + L14 `y` between them). ✅ Do **not** add a third architecture verb before L20.
- **Too many question layers?** Only **one** (L12). The full Q-system is reserved. ✅ **Do not** add a second question layer in this band (that was Option B's mistake).
- **Too many tense/aspect/mood doorways?** Only **one**, and it's a **preview** (L18), not an owned doorway. ✅ Past tense stays out entirely.
- **Too much vocabulary expansion?** Only **L17** (feelings) is vocabulary-forward, and it must stay *in frames* (thematic archetype QA risk). ✅ Watch for an emotional-lexis dump.
- **Too much café/travel context?** The band must **continue the L6→L10 café reduction**; after-class / help / home / class scenes, not café orders or travel itineraries. Flag: `y`-light (L14) and futur preview (L18) tempt travel framing — **keep human, not touristic**.
- **Too little novelty after integration lessons?** **The standing risk** (L6/L10 both flagged it). With 4 integration/milestone beats in the band, L13/L16/L19 must each carry a **forward preview hook** (per the archetype #10 rule) — L13→`y`, L16→devoir/ASM, L19→futur/Campfire — to avoid feeling like repeated quizzes.
