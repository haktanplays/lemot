# Le Mot Lesson Archetype Templates v1

> **Purpose**: This document defines **archetype-weighted lesson templates**. It sits **between** the global `docs/syllabus/lesson-spec-template-v1.1.md` (the universal shape every lesson fills) and the individual lesson specs (`docs/syllabus/L0N-*.lesson-spec.md`). Its job is to stop future lesson specs from becoming **fully bespoke** while avoiding **one rigid formula**. Planning/spec layer only — authorizes **no** code, content, flag, or runtime change.

> **Authority**: Follows `docs/learning-engine-v1.md` (pedagogical canon) and the global lesson template. Where this and locked product canon (v1 Canon, Q1–Q6, D1–D6, the locked L0→L1→L2 spine) disagree, **locked canon wins**.

> **The 70 / 20 / 10 model.** The target is:
> - **~70% template-driven** — the global spine and template fields carry the lesson.
> - **~20% archetype-weighted** — the archetype below tunes *weighting, depth, item budget, QA risks, and output metadata*.
> - **~10% truly bespoke** — only when a lesson genuinely needs it, **with explicit justification** (§13).
>
> An archetype **never replaces the spine.** Every lesson still runs the global ten sections:
> **Meet It → Notice the Pieces → Why This Works → Try It → Weave It → Shape It → Say It Your Way → Natural Reveal → Stay With It → Lesson End.**
> The archetype only changes *how much weight each section gets, how big the item budget is, which risks to watch, and what the lesson outputs downstream.*

> **How to read each archetype.** Each block lists: when to use · teaches best · section weighting (which spine sections are **strong** vs **light**) · typical item budget · typical active/supported/recognition split · carry-in/carry-out behavior · Mon Lexique behavior · Practice Pool behavior · Daily Review behavior · Sound/Writing behavior · French-specific contrast behavior · QA risks · likely lessons. Budgets are **planning targets, not validators** (engine §7).

## 1. Chunk / Natural Speech / Survival

- **When to use**: early social/survival lessons; any lesson whose value is *usable human moments* rather than a grammar system.
- **Teaches best**: social phrases, polite requests, rescue phrases, natural-speech chunks, "first real interaction" confidence.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Meet It, Opening Sentence Family, Say It Your Way, Natural Reveal. **Light** — Why This Works (minimal grammar), Shape It (limited transformation).
- **Item budget**: total ~30–40. **Active-new ~8–12** (low end is correct here — chunk-dense, per template §6). Production targets ~4–6.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 30% / supported 25% / recognition 35% / recycled 10% (recognition-heavy on social/register/culture).
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: light carry-in early (little exists yet); carry-out seeds the next engine (e.g. `je voudrais` → `je voudrais être`).
- **Mon Lexique**: many *simple* learner-facing entries (greetings, polite formulas); keep notes plain.
- **Practice Pool**: Build-heavy (assemble known chunks); light Stretch.
- **Daily Review**: frequent short hooks; the social loop resurfaces often.
- **Sound/Writing**: 0–1 minor note only (e.g. silent finals); never a pronunciation lecture.
- **French-specific contrast**: register contrasts (`je voudrais` vs `je veux`); light.
- **QA risks**: phrasebook list · too many fixed expressions · too little generative frame · **active count inflated by social chunks** (use a frame to carry the active load).
- **Likely lessons**: **L1 Survival Kit (pilot)**; later situational/social refreshers.

## 2. Architecture Verb

- **When to use**: lesson target is a major verb that must become a *production engine*.
- **Teaches best**: turning a verb (être, avoir, aller, faire, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir/falloir) into a reusable shape — **not** a conjugation table.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Engine Plan, Opening Sentence Family, Why This Works (micro-contrast), Shape It. **Light** — exhaustive person-form coverage (deliberately not pursued).
- **Item budget**: total ~35–45. **Active-new ~10–14.** Production targets ~5–7.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 25–30% / supported 25–30% / recognition 30% / recycled 15%.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: grows the prior engine (e.g. L1 `je voudrais` → `je voudrais être`); carries out to negation/question (L3), avoir-contrast (L4), tense doorways (later auxiliary/imparfait).
- **Mon Lexique**: the verb + one active person-form as core entries; other forms recognition-tagged.
- **Practice Pool**: Build + Stretch on the dominant frame; Challenge = subject/slot transformation.
- **Daily Review**: the engine frame returns often; the verb is load-bearing.
- **Sound/Writing**: 0–1 note (e.g. silent `est`, liaison); recognition, not drill.
- **French-specific contrast**: high — profession-article, `c'est` vs `il/elle est`, être-vs-avoir seed.
- **QA risks** (per template §4 guardrail): **full paradigm drilling · conjugation-table smell · too many person-forms active · too little real production.** Keep **one dominant active core**.
- **Likely lessons**: **L2 Être (pilot)**; later avoir, aller, faire, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir/falloir.

## 3. Negation / Question / Social Choice

- **When to use**: lesson teaches saying no, yes/no control, choosing tu/vous, asking/answering simple questions.
- **Teaches best**: control of polarity and register; short sentence transformations of known engines.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Why This Works (micro-contrast), Try It (trap repair), Shape It (transformations). **Light** — new vocabulary intake.
- **Item budget**: total ~30–40. **Active-new ~8–12.** Production targets ~5–7 (mostly transformations).
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 30% / supported 30% / recognition 30% / recycled 10% — transformation-heavy, low new lexis.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: high carry-in (negation/question *operate on* L1–L2 engines: `je suis` → `je ne suis pas` / `vous êtes ?`); carries out register awareness and question-forming to later lessons.
- **Mon Lexique**: few new entries; mostly *updates* (e.g. `je suis` gains a negation form; `tu`/`vous` gain register notes).
- **Practice Pool**: transformation-heavy (affirmative↔negative, statement↔question); trap repair.
- **Daily Review**: mixed polarity/register retrieval of known engines.
- **Sound/Writing**: intonation of questions (1 note max); `ne`-drop in speech (recognition).
- **French-specific contrast**: yes/no answer patterns, tu/vous register, `si` vs `oui` (recognition).
- **QA risks**: too many abstract grammar labels · too much social explanation · **overloading tu/vous + negation + question formation in one lesson** (split if it bloats).
- **Likely lessons**: **L3 Negation / Yes-No / Tu-Vous (pilot)**.

> **L3 pilot guardrail** (from `docs/syllabus/L03-negation-you-questions.lesson-spec.md`):
> - **Own one dominant transformation target, not the whole grammar system.** For an early L3-style lesson, that target is **`ne…pas`**.
> - **`ne…plus` / `ne…jamais` / `ne…rien` are recognition-only or deferred** unless a later lesson explicitly promotes them.
> - **Yes-no intonation belongs here** as the first question doorway. **Full question formation — `est-ce que` expansion, question words, inversion — is deferred** to later question-focused lessons.
> - **Tu/vous = social choice using already-known verbs/forms** (e.g. `tu es` / `vous êtes`), **not** an excuse to expand conjugation.
> - **Do not pull in prerequisite-unsafe verbs** just to demonstrate grammar.
> - **Avoid partitive / object-negation overload** — use safe alternatives like `ça` when needed and leave article pressure for the object/article lesson.

## 4. Avoir-State / Human State

- **When to use**: lesson teaches French "have"-states and the être/avoir boundary.
- **Teaches best**: `j'ai faim`, `j'ai besoin de`, `j'ai envie de`, `j'ai peur`; the contrast with être.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Why This Works (French-specific contrast), Opening Sentence Family, Say It Your Way (emotionally useful). **Light** — abstract grammar.
- **Item budget**: total ~30–42. **Active-new ~10–13.** Production targets ~5–7.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 30% / supported 25% / recognition 30% / recycled 15% (être carry-in is heavy).
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: strong carry-in from L2 (`je suis fatigué` sits beside `j'ai faim`); carries out to article system (L5: `j'ai un …`) and later idiomatic avoir.
- **Mon Lexique**: avoir + its state chunks; each entry links to its être counterpart where one exists.
- **Practice Pool**: contrast repair (être vs avoir), state-chunk Build/Stretch.
- **Daily Review**: alternate être-state and avoir-state prompts to keep the boundary live.
- **Sound/Writing**: elision (`j'ai`); 1 note max.
- **French-specific contrast**: **central** — "I am hungry" → `j'ai faim` (not *je suis faim*), the literal-transfer trap (template §7.10).
- **QA risks**: **literal English "I am hungry" transfer** · too many avoir chunks at once · **not enough continuity with être** (the contrast is the point — keep L2 present).
- **Likely lessons**: likely **L4**.

## 5. Object / Article / Noun-System

- **When to use**: lesson introduces the article/object system (un/une/le/la/les/des) and noun slots.
- **Teaches best**: gender/number **as usage**, not as a taxonomy lecture; filling object slots in known frames.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Notice the Pieces (slot logic), Weave It / Shape It (slot replacement), visual contrast. **Light** — exhaustive article tables.
- **Item budget**: total ~32–42. **Active-new ~10–13** (nouns + article usage). Production targets ~5–6.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 30% / supported 25% / recognition 30% / recycled 15%.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: carry-in `je voudrais ___` / `c'est un …` (L1/L2 frames already hold articles); carries out toward pronouns (L6: le/la/les → object pronouns) and quantity.
- **Mon Lexique**: nouns enter **with their article/gender** baked into the example, not as a separate gender field shown to the learner.
- **Practice Pool**: slot replacement (swap nouns in a frame); gender-pair listening.
- **Daily Review**: noun-in-frame retrieval; avoid bare flashcard nouns.
- **Sound/Writing**: elision (`l'`), liaison (`les_amis`); 1 note max.
- **French-specific contrast**: article presence/absence (profession no-article callback), `un` vs `le`.
- **QA risks**: **article-taxonomy lecture** · too many nouns without production frames · **gender overload** (show, don't drill).
- **Likely lessons**: likely **L5**.

## 6. Pronoun / Particle

- **When to use**: lessons on y, en, le/la/les, lui/leur, reflexives.
- **Teaches best**: slot logic, replacement logic, meaning compression (a pronoun stands in for a known phrase).
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Notice the Pieces (visual slotting), Why This Works (micro-contrast), Try It (repair + listening traps). **Light** — early free production.
- **Item budget**: total ~30–40. **Active-new ~8–11** (pronouns are few but dense). Production targets ~4–6.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 25% / supported 30% / recognition 35% / recycled 10% — **recognition before production** is essential here.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: heavy carry-in (pronouns replace *already-known* nouns/phrases); carries out to richer sentence compression.
- **Mon Lexique**: each pronoun entry shows *what it replaces* with a before/after example.
- **Practice Pool**: replacement transforms (full phrase → pronoun), listening traps (`le`/`les`, `lui`/`leur`).
- **Daily Review**: replacement retrieval; pair with the noun it stands for.
- **Sound/Writing**: liaison and elision are frequent here; 1 note.
- **French-specific contrast**: word-order shift vs English (pronoun before verb); position of `y`/`en`.
- **QA risks**: **too abstract too early** · **pronoun stacking overload** · **production before recognition** (recognize first).
- **Likely lessons**: mid-spine pronoun lessons (e.g. L6+).

## 7. Tense / Aspect / Mood Doorway

- **When to use**: lesson opens a new time/aspect/mood for *already-known* engines.
- **Teaches best**: transforming old engines across time (futur proche, passé composé, imparfait, être en train de, venir de, futur simple, conditionnel, subjonctif doorway, imperative).
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Shape It (old-engine transformation), Why This Works (timeline meaning), Opening Sentence Family, Natural Reveal (alternatives). **Light** — new lexis.
- **Item budget**: total ~32–42. **Active-new ~8–12** (the *form* is the new thing, not many words). Production targets ~5–7 transformations.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 25% / supported 30% / recognition 35% / recycled 10% — many forms first appear as **chunks/recognition** (engine §10).
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: very high carry-in (the doorway *needs* prior engines to transform); carries out to fuller tense control.
- **Mon Lexique**: known items gain a new tense/aspect form; show the timeline, not a paradigm.
- **Practice Pool**: present↔new-tense transforms; timeline ordering.
- **Daily Review**: re-tense known sentences; spaced reappearance of the new form.
- **Sound/Writing**: form-specific endings (1 note); recognition.
- **French-specific contrast**: aspect contrasts (passé composé vs imparfait), `aller`+inf vs futur simple — at the right level.
- **QA risks**: **heavy grammar lecture · too many forms · too little usage context.** Open *one* doorway; chunk-first.
- **Likely lessons**: the tense/aspect/mood ladder lessons across the spine.

## 8. Pattern Transfer Verb

- **When to use**: lesson teaches a *predictable verb family* so the learner can generalize, not memorize each verb.
- **Teaches best**: transfer/prediction (regular -er family, predictable groups); "learn the pattern, unlock many verbs."
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Why This Works (the pattern), Notice the Pieces (stem + ending), Shape It (apply to new verbs). **Light** — exhaustive per-verb coverage.
- **Item budget**: total ~32–42. **Active-new few verbs (~4–6 active verbs)** but many recognition verbs (~10–15). Production targets ~5–6.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 20% / supported 25% / recognition 45% / recycled 10% — **many recognition verbs, fewer active verbs**.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: carry-in the subject/pronoun system; carries out the *prediction skill* to all future regular verbs.
- **Mon Lexique**: a few model verbs as full entries; the rest tagged as "follows the -er pattern" (recognition).
- **Practice Pool**: apply-the-pattern transforms (new verb, same ending); prediction drills framed as discovery.
- **Daily Review**: rotate different -er verbs through the same frame.
- **Sound/Writing**: silent endings (`-ent`); 1 note.
- **French-specific contrast**: English "to ___" → French stem+ending; false-regular traps.
- **QA risks**: **word list · conjugation table · too many verbs active** (teach the pattern, recognize the verbs).
- **Likely lessons**: regular -er lesson and later verb-group expansions.

## 9. Thematic Vocabulary / Context

- **When to use**: lesson grows vocabulary inside a theme (food, city, family, home, work, body, feelings).
- **Teaches best**: usable vocabulary **inside frames**, not isolated word lists.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Opening Sentence Family (frames the theme), Mon Lexique output, Practice Pool seeds. **Light** — new grammar (rides existing engines).
- **Item budget**: total ~35–45. **Active-new ~10–14** (theme words **in frames**). Production targets ~5–6.
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 30% / supported 25% / recognition 30% / recycled 15%.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: carry-in known frames (`je voudrais ___`, `c'est ___`, `il/elle est ___`) to host the new vocabulary; carries out richer thematic expression.
- **Mon Lexique**: the lesson's main output — rich themed entries, each with a where-used frame example.
- **Practice Pool**: frame-filling with theme words; scenario cards.
- **Daily Review**: themed retrieval inside frames.
- **Sound/Writing**: theme-specific note only if natural (1 max).
- **French-specific contrast**: theme false-friends, gendered theme nouns.
- **QA risks**: **vocabulary dump · too little grammar/engine continuity · low production** (every word needs a frame to live in).
- **Likely lessons**: thematic lessons across the spine.

## 10. Review / Integration

- **When to use**: periodic consolidation; no major new system.
- **Teaches best**: growing old engines, mixed production, weak-point repair, cumulative Weave.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — Weave It, Natural Reveal, Stay With It (Practice-Pool-style items). **Light** — Meet It / Why This Works (little new to meet or explain).
- **Item budget**: total ~25–35. **Active-new ~0–4** (mostly recycled). Production targets ~6–8 (volume of *retrieval*, not novelty).
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 10% / supported 20% / recognition 15% / **recycled 55%** — recycle-dominant by design.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: maximal carry-in across recent lessons; carries out consolidated, faded-to-active items.
- **Mon Lexique**: mostly *updates* (mastery events, strengthened entries); few or no new entries.
- **Practice Pool**: the core of the lesson — Build/Stretch/Challenge across recent engines; weak-point repair.
- **Daily Review**: this lesson *is* a structured review; feeds spaced retrieval forward.
- **Sound/Writing**: revisit a prior note only if a weak point demands it.
- **French-specific contrast**: re-test prior contrasts that caused errors.
- **QA risks**: **feels like a test · too little novelty · reward/gamification language** (it must still feel like calm practice, never a graded exam — no streak/XP/score).
- **Likely lessons**: periodic integration lessons (e.g. before a milestone).

## 11. Milestone / Transition

- **When to use**: a gate or transition (Campfire gate, Summit transition, French-first readiness, Core final).
- **Teaches best**: assess + reflect + transition; proving the learner can produce, and naming who they've become.
- **Section weighting**: **Strong** — production proof (Say It Your Way / Weave), passive mirror, learner identity, recap (Lesson End). **Light** — new teaching (a milestone teaches almost nothing new).
- **Item budget**: total ~20–35. **Active-new ~0–3.** Production targets ~5–8 (proof of prior learning).
- **Active/supported/recognition split**: roughly active 10% / supported 15% / recognition 10% / **recycled 65%**.
- **Carry-in / carry-out**: pure carry-in (proves the milestone's scope); carries out a *threshold* (e.g. Campfire gate → premium path).
- **Mon Lexique**: reflection view — surfaces what the learner now owns; mastery summary, no new entries.
- **Practice Pool**: a curated proof set, not a fresh pool.
- **Daily Review**: may pause or shift mode at the transition.
- **Sound/Writing**: none new.
- **French-specific contrast**: none new; may surface a "you can now hear/say X" reflection.
- **QA risks**: **ceremony too theatrical · XP/streak/reward language · too much marketing copy.** Passive mirror only; a milestone is a calm acknowledgement, not a celebration screen. (Respects DEV_APK_MVP_CANON copy rules and the Campfire-at-L24 paywall framing.)
- **Likely lessons**: Campfire gate (L24), Summit transition, Core final, monolingual-readiness transitions.

## 12. Combining primary + secondary archetypes

Most real lessons are **one primary archetype + at most one secondary**:

- The **primary** archetype sets the section weighting, the item budget, and the dominant QA risks.
- The **secondary** archetype contributes a *minor* weighting nudge and one or two of its risks to watch — it does **not** double the budget.
- **L2 Être** is the worked example: primary **Architecture Verb**, with a light **French-specific-contrast** flavor (profession article, c'est vs il est) drawn from archetype 4's contrast discipline — without becoming an avoir-state lesson.

Rules for combining:
1. **Pick one primary.** Its budget and weighting win.
2. **At most one secondary**, and only if it earns its place. If you need two secondaries, the lesson is overloaded — split it.
3. **Budgets do not add.** A primary + secondary lesson still respects the *primary's* item budget; the secondary borrows a slot or two, it doesn't expand the total.
4. **Risks accumulate, not weighting.** Carry the secondary's QA risks into §15, but don't let it pull the spine weighting in two directions.
5. **Never combine two full-cycle engines** just because two archetypes apply (L1 pilot finding). One dominant core.

## 13. Truly bespoke lessons

A lesson is **bespoke** only when no archetype (or primary+secondary combination) fits its pedagogical job.

- **Bespoke is the ~10% exception, not a convenience.** Reaching for bespoke because filling the template feels constraining is the wrong reason.
- **Bespoke lessons require explicit justification.** The spec must state, in §15/Pilot Findings or a dedicated note: *why no archetype fits*, *what the lesson does instead*, and *which guardrails (no gamification, no conjugation table, prerequisite-safety, Mon Lexique compatibility) still apply.*
- **Even a bespoke lesson keeps the global spine** (the ten sections) and the engine-doc guardrails. Bespoke changes *weighting and content shape*, never the no-gamification identity, the passive-mirror tone, or downstream compatibility.
- If a "bespoke" pattern recurs across 2–3 lessons, it is no longer bespoke — **promote it to a new archetype** in a future version of this document.
