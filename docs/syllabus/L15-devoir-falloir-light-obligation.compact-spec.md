## 2. Owned Target

**Asymmetric, two light obligation forms over owned infinitives.**

**Primary ‚Äî `il faut + inf.`** (active, impersonal, **invariable**) ‚Äî "it's necessary / we need to":
- `Il faut faire une pause.`
- `Il faut aller √† la maison.`
- `Il faut r√©p√©ter.` *(light ‚Äî `r√©p√©ter` owned from the rescue chunk; statement only)*
- **`Il faut m'aider.` ‚Äî DELIBERATELY AVOIDED**: `m'` is the object pronoun frozen inside L11's `vous pouvez m'aider ?`; putting it after `il faut` stresses the object pronoun **outside** its frozen chunk. Keep `m'aider` only in `vous pouvez m'aider`; **not owned here.**

**Supported ‚Äî `je dois + inf.`** (one person-form) ‚Äî personal "I have to / I must":
- `Je dois faire √ßa.`
- `Je dois aller √† la maison.`
- `Je dois faire une pause.`

**Clarify:**
- `il faut` = general "it's necessary / we need to" (impersonal, no subject).
- `je dois` = personal "I have to / I need to" (parallels `je peux`).
- **Keep the contrast light** ‚Äî a one-line feel ("impersonal vs personal", "can vs must"), **not** a full obligation-nuance lecture. The only genuinely new lexis is the two modal forms; infinitives are owned.

## 3. Not Owned / Deferred (explicit)

Recognition-only or deferred ‚Äî never production targets in L15:
- **full `devoir` paradigm**; **`tu dois` / `vous devez` / `on doit`** = recognition only (no production).
- **`il faut que`** + the **subjunctive** (`il faut` takes **no `que`-clause** here).
- **conditionnel / advice**: `je devrais` ("I should") / `il faudrait` ("one should") ‚Äî L15 owns obligation, not advice.
- **`devoir` = "to owe"** (`je dois 5 euros`).
- **`il me faut`** (need + pronoun) and **`il faut + noun`** ownership (`il faut une pause`).
- **broad advice / moral obligation**; **past / future** (`j'ai d√ª`, `il a fallu`, `je devrai`).
- **`y` + devoir chains** (`je dois y aller`).
- **pouvoir/devoir contrast as a lecture** (a light feel is fine; a nuance lecture is not).

## 5. Sentence Family (after-class / obligation scene; owned infinitives only)

> Scene: *"You're tired. One must take a break; you have to go home ‚Äî but first there's something you must do."* The can‚Üîmust / impersonal‚Üîpersonal contrasts are the spine.

| Role | Sentence | Note |
|---|---|---|

## 9. Natural Reveal / Feedback

**Obligation (signature):**
- **Expected:** `Il faut faire une pause.` / `Je dois aller √† la maison.`
- **Acceptable alternatives:** `Je dois faire une pause.` (personal) for `Il faut faire une pause.` (impersonal) ‚Äî both fine; note the personal/impersonal nuance lightly.

**Common mistakes (watch list):**
- **`je faut`** (using `faut` with `je`) ‚Äî `il faut` is **impersonal**: only `il faut`; for "I", use `je dois`.
- **`il dois`** (using `dois` with `il`) ‚Äî `je dois` is personal; `il` takes `il faut` (or, later, `il doit`).
- **`il faut que` + subjunctive** (`il faut que tu fasses‚Ä¶`) ‚Äî the subjunctive is **later**; hint: "for now, `il faut` + the plain verb: `il faut **faire**`." (`trap:il-faut-que-too-early` / `trap:subjunctive-too-early`)
- **`je devrais` / `il faudrait`** ("should") ‚Äî that's **advice**, a softer later form. (`trap:conditionnel-advice-too-early`)
- **`je dois 5 euros`** (owe) ‚Äî here `devoir` = must, not owe. (`trap:devoir-owe-too-early`)
- **`il me faut` / `il faut une pause`** ‚Äî keep the verb: `il faut **faire** une pause`. (`trap:il-me-faut-too-early`)
- **`je dois y aller`** ‚Äî `y` + devoir is later; for now `je dois aller √† la maison`. (`trap:je-dois-y-aller-too-early`)
- **over-moralizing / advice tone** ‚Äî L15 is concrete obligation, not life advice; passive mirror only.

> Feedback stays passive-mirror; no scores/reward/advice tone. Recognition forms (`il faut que`, `je devrais`, paradigm, `je dois y aller`) are **redirected, never taught on the spot** (contract ¬ß11).

## 12. QA / Pilot Findings
