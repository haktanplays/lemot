> **Why this gate exists.** The band map (Option C) pencils **L14 = "`y` as place pronoun (light doorway)"** — the spine's **first pronoun**. Pronouns are the archetype-#6 surface most prone to two failures: **"production before recognition"** and **"too abstract too early."** Before writing the spec, this review fixes the safest L14 scope so `y` cannot balloon into the full French pronoun/replacement system or leak `en`.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Should L14 be `y`-light?** **Yes — but chunk-first**, not as a productive pronoun rule. Own **`j'y vais` / `on y va`** (place `y`) as high-frequency near-fixed expressions, **recognition-first**, with the "`y` = the place you already named" insight shown and **lightly** practiced. `je n'y vais pas` is **supported/optional**; `j'y suis` (extending `y` to être) is **recognition only**.
- **What exactly should it own?** `chunk:j-y-vais` (graduated from the L13 recognition hook) + `chunk:on-y-va`, plus `phen:y-place-pronoun-doorway` at recognition→supported depth, and a **light** `frame:subject-y-aller` (the `je/on/tu + y + aller` shape). Production stays minimal (the two-three expressions).
- **What must remain deferred?** **`en`** (paid-zone) and the **`y`/`en` contrast**; **`il y a`** (existential — separate later doorway, recognition-only at most, **must not be conflated** with place-`y`); the **object-pronoun system** + **stacking**; **`y` with multiple verbs / broad `à`-replacement**; **`y` + `est-ce que`** production; the **`y` + pouvoir chain** (`je peux y aller`); full pronoun placement as a rule; location prepositions; futur proche.
- **Is L14 safe after L13?** **Yes**, on three conditions: **(a)** chunk-first (not a productive system), **(b)** **recognition-first** (the archetype-#6 non-negotiable), **(c)** followed by the rhythm (L14 `y` + L15 devoir → **L16 integration**). L13 already seeded `j'y vais` as recognition, so L14 *lands a hook* rather than opening cold.

> **Headline recommendation (product-critical, not flattering the plan):** the band map's "place `y` only" is right, but **"doorway" overstates it** — L14 should own `y` as **two-three near-chunks**, not a doorway to a productive replacement rule. Pronouns are the spine's first genuinely abstract surface (word-order shift: `j'y vais`, not `je vais y`); a productive rule at lesson 14 hits both archetype-#6 traps. Own the expressions, defer the system.

## 3. Candidate L14 Ownership Options

### Option A — Chunk-first `y`-light (RECOMMENDED)
**Own:** `j'y vais` / `on y va` as high-frequency near-fixed expressions; `je n'y vais pas` **supported/optional**; the "`y` = there / to that place you named" insight, lightly practiced.
**Scope:** `y` as "there / to that place" in a **few high-frequency chunks** (tied to *aller*-movement), recognition-first.
**Defer:** full `y` pronoun grammar · `y` replacing **every** `à + place` · `y` with **multiple verbs** (`j'y suis`, `j'y pense`) · the `y`/`en` contrast · object-pronoun stacking.
- **Strengths:** lands the L13 hook; the expressions are everyday real speech (high utility for one lesson); the abstract *system* stays closed; word-order learned **inside** the fixed expressions; fits archetype-#6 recognition-first.
- **Risks:** over-generalization to `en` / other senses / full rule — guarded by traps + recognition-first.
- **Verdict:** **adopt.**

### Option B — `y` as a full place-pronoun doorway
**Own:** `y` replaces `à + place` **generally** — e.g. `Je vais à la maison → J'y vais`, **`Je suis à Paris → J'y suis`**.
- **Too broad.** Generalizing across verbs (`aller` *and* `être`) and across any `à + place` forces productive word-order manipulation at lesson 14 — the **"too abstract too early" + "production before recognition"** trap head-on. It also makes `en` ("replace `de + X`") the obvious next rule, threatening paid-depth. `j'y suis` is exactly the Option-B over-reach: keep it **recognition only**.
- **Verdict:** **reject for L14.** The productive replacement rule belongs to a **later** pronoun lesson, once the chunks are automatic.

### Option C — No `y` ownership yet (integration instead)
- Keep `j'y vais` recognition and make L14 an integration/review beat.
- **Rejected:** L13 was already the integration beat; L16 is the next one. Another integration at L14 over-integrates and **dangles the L13 hook**. Place-`y` is free-tier-appropriate (band map §5) — deferring gains nothing (the deep value, `en` + the full system, stays deferred regardless).

**Recommendation: Option A** (chunk-first `y`-light, recognition-first).

## 6. Paywall / Commercial Depth Check

- **Does owning `y`-light before Campfire ~L24 give away too much?** **No.** `j'y vais` / `on y va` are two everyday expressions, not the pronoun system. The deep, broadly-applicable surface — **`en`, the object-pronoun system (`le`/`la`/`lui`/`leur`…) + stacking, and the productive replacement rule** — stays **entirely reserved** (band map §5: place-`y` early-free; `en` + others paid-zone). Pronoun analogue of L11 (pouvoir help-slice) and L12 (est-ce que yes/no-slice).
- **Does withholding `y` make the free path weaker?** **Yes, slightly — `j'y vais` is iconic real French.** A learner who can't say "let's go / I'm off" feels stuck below natural speech. Owning the chunks is a satisfying idiomatic win without spending the system.
- **Safest commercial compromise:** **own `j'y vais` / `on y va` (place `y`) as near-chunks + light replacement; defer `en`, the contrast, object pronouns/stacking, and the productive rule.**

**ID issues flagged:**
- **Apostrophe stripping:** `j'y` → `j-y` (convention §2/§3: apostrophe → hyphen, ASCII). `chunk:j-y-vais` is already ASCII-clean ✓; learner-facing label keeps `j'y vais`.
- **`y` as letter / word / pronoun (homograph):** the brief lists `word:y`, but `y` has **multiple senses** (place pronoun, `il y a` existential, `j'y pense` `à`-pronoun, the letter). **Recommend the sense-suffix `word:y-place`** (convention §2 homograph rule, like `ou-where`/`la-there`), reserving `word:y-*` for other senses. The band map §9 already flagged the `y` homograph guard — **apply it here.**
- **`il y a` as chunk vs `y`-pronoun system:** treat `il y a` as a **frozen `chunk:il-y-a`** (existential), **distinct** from `word:y-place`. Do not let the L14 spec imply they share a system.
- **Graduation precedent:** L14 is the **second** lesson to graduate a recognition item to active via `status_by_lesson` (after L12's `est-ce que je peux`) — reinforces that convention §6 mechanism (worth a one-line convention note when the spec is written).

## 10. Final Verdict

- **Is L14 ready for detailed spec writing?** **Yes — ready for a *compact* spec**, once the three §9 gates are locked.
- **What should L14 own?** **`j'y vais` / `on y va` (place `y`)** as recognition-first near-chunks (+ supported optional `je n'y vais pas`, + light `tu y vas ?`); graduate the L13 `j'y vais` hook.
- **What should L14 absolutely NOT own?** **`en`** + the `y`/`en` contrast; **`il y a`** as a system (separate doorway); the **object-pronoun system + stacking**; **`y` with multiple verbs** (`j'y suis` = recognition) or **broad `à`-replacement**; **`y` + `est-ce que`** / **`y` + pouvoir** production; full pronoun placement as a rule; location prepositions; futur proche. Full pronoun-system ownership = 0; new systems = 1 narrow doorway.
- **Single highest-risk leak:** the **pronoun system over-opening from `y`** — specifically **`en` + the object-pronoun system (+ stacking)** produced because `y` *feels* like it unlocks "French pronouns," compounded by the **production-before-recognition** trap and **word-order errors** (`je vais y`). Prerequisite-safety + cognitive-load risk; AI generation and over-eager production are the likely vectors.
