> **PILOT.** Second **Architecture Verb** opening after L7 (aller), built as a **split-sense / small-action opening** (archetype #2 L7 guardrail). It opens **`faire`** on **one narrow sense only — doing/making a *small action*, especially *taking a pause*** — and **holds back** faire's huge universe (weather, sport, idioms, `se faire`, the full paradigm). It deepens the human scene from L6 and reuses the owned `je voudrais + infinitive`, `ne…pas`, and `j'ai besoin de` frames. §18 records the findings.

> **Archetype**: primary = **Architecture Verb** (#2), as a **split-sense small-action opening**. Secondary (flavor only, not a second budget) = **Chunk / Natural Speech / Human Expression** (#1). **New grammar systems: 0. Full `faire` ownership: 0.**

## Canon Alignment Note (read first)

Spine: **L0 → L1 → L2 → L3 → L4 → L5 → L6 → L7 → L8 → L9 (this)**. L6 gave human context, L7 gave movement (`je vais à + place`), L8 gave the `où` where-control. The learner can now describe states, needs, movement, and ask *where* — but cannot yet talk about **doing a small action**. `faire` is the natural next engine, but it is enormous; L9 opens only the **small-action / pause** slice.

**Split-sense opening (uses the L7 archetype guardrail).** Just as L7 owned `aller` for *movement* and held futur proche back, **L9 owns `faire` for *small actions / taking a pause* and holds back** the broad faire universe (weather `il fait froid`, sport `faire du sport`, idioms `faire attention`, `se faire` / `ça se fait`, and the full conjugation paradigm) — all **recognition only**. This protects pedagogy (prerequisite-safety) and **commercial depth** (the full faire surface stays for later lessons).

**What L9 is *not*:**
- **not** a full `faire` architecture-verb lesson (one dominant active core — the *small-action* sense via infinitive — the rest recognition);
- **not** a do/make taxonomy lesson;
- **not** a weather lesson (`il fait froid / il fait beau` = recognition only);
- **not** a sport/activity vocabulary lesson (`faire du sport` = recognition; no activity dump);
- **not** a `se faire` / `ça se fait` lesson (deferred);
- **not** a question-formation lesson (`qu'est-ce que tu fais ?` = recognition; `tu fais quoi ?` is a **fixed supported chunk**, not the `quoi` question-word system — see §2);
- **not** a `vouloir` lesson (`je veux` present = recognition only; the owned refusals reuse `je voudrais` / `faire` — see §2).

**Prerequisite-safety on "not wanting to do that" (explicit).** The spine owns `je voudrais` (conditional, L1) but **not** `je veux` (present `vouloir`). So L9's owned refusals are **`je ne fais pas ça`** (pure `faire` + `ne…pas`, fully safe) and **`je ne voudrais pas faire ça`** (reuses the owned `je voudrais`). **`je ne veux pas faire ça` is recognition only** — extremely common in speech, so it's *shown*, but `veux` (present vouloir) is not produced until a later vouloir/pouvoir lesson.

**`pouvoir` stays frozen.** `pouvoir` appears **only** inside the recycled rescue chunk `pouvez-vous répéter ?` (recognition since L1) — it is **not** opened as a productive system here.

**Café-centricity:** café does not appear; the scene is **rest / taking a pause after class** (human, not transactional).

**Legacy mapping divergence (flag).** `CLAUDE.md`'s legacy 24-lesson dev-apk list maps **L9 = Food & Ordering**. The **v1 syllabus spine** (this document set, the newer active canon) maps **L9 = Faire / small actions / pause**. Per the Master Pipeline precedence rule (*newer active canon > codebase canon > older active canon*), this spec follows the **v1 spine**; the legacy bracket is noted, not adopted. Runtime/dev-apk (L1–L5 only) is unaffected — planning canon ahead of runtime.

## 3. Engine Plan

> One dominant new full-cycle engine (**`faire` for a small action / pause**), two short/supported satellites, recognition-only flavor for broad faire. Architecture-verb guardrail: **one active core (the infinitive small-action use), the rest recognition.**

| Engine | Depth | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|
| **A — `faire une pause` / do a small action** | **full-cycle** (the one new engine) | The lesson's main target: talk about **taking a pause / doing a small action**. Chunk `faire une pause`; frames `frame:faire-plus-action` and (reusing L6) `frame:je-voudrais-faire-plus-noun`. Examples: *Je voudrais faire une pause. · On fait une pause ? · Je suis fatigué, je voudrais faire une pause. · J'ai besoin d'une pause.* |
| **B — `faire ça` / not doing that** | short-cycle | Reuse owned `ne…pas` + `faire` (+ the owned `je voudrais`) to **refuse / decline a small action**: `frame:ne-pas-faire-ca`. Examples: *Je ne fais pas ça. · Je ne voudrais pas faire ça.* (Recognition only: *Je ne veux pas faire ça.* — present `vouloir` not owned.) Introduces `word:ca`. |
| **C — Action question (light)** | **supported / recognition** | A single fixed, spoken question chunk reusing L8's end-placement: `chunk:tu-fais-quoi` (*Tu fais quoi ?*). **Not** the question-word system — `quoi` is learned **inside this chunk only**. `qu'est-ce que tu fais ?` is **recognition only**. |
| **Recognition — broad `faire` (future hook)** | **recognition only** | The learner **meets, never produces**: weather `il fait froid` / `il fait beau`; `faire du sport`; `faire attention`; the full paradigm (`tu fais`, `il fait`, `vous faites`, `ils font`); and that `faire` reaches much further later. **No production required.** |

**Why this is not a full `faire` lesson.** Only the **small-action / pause** sense carries active weight, and chiefly through the **infinitive** (`je voudrais faire une pause`), which reuses the owned L6 frame. `je fais` / `on fait` are supported; weather, sport, idioms, `se faire`, and the full paradigm are recognition or deferred (§2). The teaching surface is **a human pause scene + a couple of frames**, never a conjugation grid or a do/make taxonomy.

**Why this follows L6–L8 naturally.** The tired-after-class scene from L6–L8 is exactly where "I need a break / I'd like to take a pause" belongs. L9 reuses `je suis fatigué` (L6), `j'ai besoin de` (L4), `je voudrais + infinitive` (L6), `ne…pas` (L3), and the L8 spoken end-placement — adding one verb (`faire`) and two nouns/words (`pause`, `ça`). It is a small step, not a new system.

**Why this does not become a do/make taxonomy.** L9 never contrasts "do vs make" or lists faire's domains. It owns `faire une pause` / `faire ça` as **usable human chunks**; the broad meanings are recognition hooks, explicitly capped.

## 4. Opening Sentence Family

> **Human scene, not a do/make list.** *"You're tired after class. You know where you're going — but right now you want to **take a pause**, and you **don't want to do that** (whatever's being asked)."*

| Role | Sentence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Open** | `Bonjour, je suis fatigué.` | L1 greeting + L6 state (recycled) |
| **Need (recycled + new noun)** | `J'ai besoin d'une pause.` | L4 `j'ai besoin de` + `une pause` (new) |
| **Want a pause (anchor)** | `Je voudrais faire une pause.` | **new core**: `je voudrais` + `faire une pause` |
| **Suggest a pause** | `On fait une pause ?` | `on fait` (supported) + rising intonation |
| **Refuse (faire)** | `Je ne fais pas ça.` | **new core**: `je fais` (supported) + `ne…pas` + `ça` |
| **Refuse (polite, owned)** | `Je ne voudrais pas faire ça.` | reuses owned `je voudrais` + `ne…pas` + `faire ça` |
| **Action question (light)** | `Tu fais quoi ?` | supported fixed chunk (L8 end-placement + `quoi`) |
| **Movement (recycled)** | `Je vais à la maison.` | L7 (recycled), light |
| **Rescue (recycled)** | `Je ne comprends pas. Pouvez-vous répéter ?` | L1 rescue, in context |
| **Reassure (recycled)** | `C'est pas grave.` | L2 chunk, natural repair |

- **Interchangeable pieces**: `je voudrais faire { une pause }` · `j'ai besoin d'{ une pause }` · `je ne { fais pas / voudrais pas faire } { ça }` · `on fait { une pause } ?`.
- **Fixed frames**: `frame:faire-plus-action` · `frame:je-voudrais-faire-plus-noun` · `frame:ne-pas-faire-ca` · `chunk:on-fait-une-pause` · `chunk:tu-fais-quoi` *(supported)*. *(Recognition only)* `frame:qu-est-ce-que-tu-fais`.
- **Replaceable slots**: the **action noun** after `faire` (closed set this lesson: `une pause`; `ça` as the generic "that"); the subject toggles `je` / `on` (supported), with `tu` only inside the fixed `tu fais quoi ?` chunk.
- **Anchor**: `Je voudrais faire une pause.` · **Variations**: `On fait une pause ?`, `J'ai besoin d'une pause.`, `Je ne fais pas ça.` · **Contrast**: `Je voudrais faire une pause.` (infinitive, owned) vs `Je ne veux pas faire ça.` (present `vouloir` — **recognition only**, shown side-by-side so the learner feels the natural refusal without owning `veux`).
- **Rescue / natural sentence**: `Je ne comprends pas. Pouvez-vous répéter ?` (+ `c'est pas grave`).
- **Forbidden / not-yet-ready substitutions**:
  - **no weather faire** (`Il fait froid` = recognition only);
  - **no sport/activity dump** (`Je fais du sport`, activity lists — recognition only, capped);
  - **no full paradigm production** (`vous faites`, `ils font` stay recognition);
  - **no `qu'est-ce que` production** (recognition only); `quoi` only inside `tu fais quoi ?`;
  - **no present `vouloir`** (`je veux …` = recognition only);
  - **no `pouvoir`** beyond the frozen `pouvez-vous répéter` chunk;
  - **no `se faire` / `ça se fait`**;
  - **no past/future system**; **no futur proche production** (still deferred from L7).

## 10. French-Specific Contrast / Transfer Trap

> Micro-contrasts only (template §7.10) — "Why This Works" / Natural Reveal fuel.

- **`faire` is not "everything do/make" today.** L9 owns only the **small action** sense (`faire une pause`, `faire ça`). Weather/sport/idioms come later.
- **"I'd like to take a pause" → `Je voudrais faire une pause.`** French says **`faire`** une pause ("do a pause"), and after `je voudrais` the verb stays **infinitive** (`faire`, not `fais`). Don't over-explain the English "take/make a break" mismatch — just show the natural French.
- **"I don't want to do that" → `Je ne fais pas ça` / `Je ne voudrais pas faire ça`.** Use owned forms; `je ne veux pas faire ça` (present `veux`) is the most natural spoken refusal but is **recognition only** here — produced after `vouloir` is taught.

## 12. Natural Reveal / Feedback Plan

**Take a pause (signature):**
- **Expected**: `Je voudrais faire une pause.`
- **Acceptable alternatives**: `J'ai besoin d'une pause.`; `On fait une pause ?`; with `s'il vous plaît`.
- **Natural upgrade**: add a reason (`je suis fatigué`).
- **Common mistakes**: **`je suis faire`** (être + faire); **`je voudrais fais`** (conjugated after `je voudrais`); `je voudrais une pause` (dropping `faire` — acceptable but note the natural `faire une pause`). **Hint**: "After `je voudrais`, keep the verb whole: `faire`. To take a break, French *does* a pause: `faire une pause`."
- **Weak-point tags**: `weak:voudrais-plus-infinitive`, `weak:faire-overbroad`.
- **Passive mirror**: "You asked for a pause."

**Refuse a small action:**
- **Expected**: `Je ne fais pas ça.` / `Je ne voudrais pas faire ça.`
- **Acceptable alternatives**: spoken `je fais pas ça` (recognised, lightly upgraded).
- **Common mistakes**: `je ne veux pas faire ça` (present `veux` — recognised, mirrored, **not corrected as wrong**: "natural — you'll own `je veux` soon; for now `je ne fais pas ça` / `je ne voudrais pas faire ça`"); misplacing `ne…pas`.
- **Weak-point**: `weak:negation-placement`.

**Suggest / action question:**
- **Expected**: `On fait une pause ?` / `Tu fais quoi ?`
- **Common mistakes**: `Qu'est-ce que tu fais ?` (est-ce que — recognition only); full-paradigm slips. **Hint**: "In speech you can just say `tu fais quoi ?`"
- **Weak-point**: `weak:faire-paradigm`, `weak:quoi-question-word`.

**Broad faire (recognition — feedback is recognition-framed, never a production correction):**
- **Shown**: `Il fait froid.` / `Je fais du sport.` — "`faire` does a lot more (weather, sport, …); you'll own those later. Today it's just small actions."
- **If the learner *attempts* weather/sport/paradigm faire**: accept the meaning, mirror gently, tag `weak:faire-overbroad`, defer — **do not** teach the broad use on the spot (contract §11).

**Common mistakes (lesson-wide watch list):** `je suis faire` · `je voudrais fais` · `je fais une pause` vs `je voudrais faire une pause` nuance · weather-faire leakage · `qu'est-ce que` overload · using `pouvoir`/present `vouloir` before taught. **Passive mirror throughout; no scores, no reward language.**

## 18. L9 Pilot Findings

**Does `faire`-light work as L9?** **Yes — on a narrow, human reading.** `faire une pause` is immediately useful and emotionally resonant in the tired-after-class scene the spine has been building since L6; it gives the learner the ability to **ask for rest** and **refuse a small action**, which is real conversational agency. The split-sense opening (proven on L7's `aller`) transfers cleanly to `faire`: own one sense, cap the rest. The lesson **works**, with one honest caveat below.

**Is it better than `pouvoir` at this point?** **Yes, marginally — because of dependency order.** You need an **action to name** before "can/may I do it?" is useful. `faire` (especially `faire ça` / `faire une pause`) gives the learner a small-action object that **`pouvoir` can then modalize** at L10 (`je peux faire ça` / `je ne peux pas faire ça` / `on peut faire une pause ?`). Opening `pouvoir` first would have little concrete to attach to. So **faire-light → pouvoir-light** is the right order.

**Should L10 be `pouvoir`-light / help & permission?** **Yes — `pouvoir`-light is the recommended L10**, scoped the same way: own `je peux` / `je ne peux pas` / `on peut … ?` + `est-ce que je peux … ?` (recognition), reusing L9's `faire ça` / `faire une pause` as the thing one *can* do. It also pairs naturally with the rescue/politeness register (`pouvez-vous répéter` already frozen-recognition) and the help scene. **But** — see balance flag.

**Does L9 keep the spine balanced after L7/L8?** **It is at the edge.** L7 (aller), L8 (où), L9 (faire) — and a likely L10 (pouvoir) — is **four consecutive new engines**. The L1–L5 retrospective and the L6 pilot both showed that a run of system-heavy lessons needs a **consolidation beat** (L6 was exactly that after L2–L5). **Strong recommendation: make L11 a Review/Integration lesson** (archetype #10) that recombines L6–L10 (movement + location + small action + permission) in human scenes, **0 new systems** — before opening anything else. Flagging now so the spine doesn't over-accelerate.

**Does L9 reveal any required patch to template / archetype / AI contract / ID convention?** Minor, **non-blocking** (propose for review, do **not** auto-apply):
- **Archetype #2 (Architecture Verb)** — the L7 "split-sense opening" note now has a **second instance** (`faire` = small-action; broad faire deferred).
