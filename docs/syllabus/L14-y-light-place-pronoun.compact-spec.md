## 2. Owned Target

**Single owned target (chunk-first):** **`y` = "there / to the place you already named"**, owned through **`j'y vais` / `on y va`** as near-fixed expressions; the replacement (`je vais à la maison` → `j'y vais`) shown and **lightly** practiced — **not** a fully productive rule.

- The insight: **`y` stands in for an owned `à + place`** (you don't repeat the place), and **`y` sits before the verb** (`j'y vais`, not *`je vais y`*).
- **Graduates the L13 hook:** `j'y vais` was recognition in L13; L14 owns it.

**Owned examples (all over owned `aller` movement):**
- `J'y vais.` (I'm off / I'm going there)
- `On y va.` (Let's go / we're going there)
- `Tu y vas ?` (Are you going there?) *(supported)*
- `Je n'y vais pas.` (I'm not going there) *(supported / optional — `ne…pas` + `y`)*

**Deliberately recognition-only (not owned here):** **`j'y suis`** (`y` with *être*, "I'm there") — extending `y` to a second verb is the productive-system over-reach (gate review §3 Option B); kept recognition so `y` stays tied to *aller*-movement first.

## 3. Not Owned / Deferred (explicit)

Recognition-only or deferred — never production targets in L14:
- **`en`** + the **`y`/`en` contrast** (`j'en ai` / `j'en veux`) — paid-zone; **not introduced at all** (no contrast to teach).
- **`il y a`** (existential "there is") — a **separate later doorway**, recognition-only at most; **kept distinct** from place-`y` (`trap:il-y-a-too-early`).
- **the object-pronoun system** (`le`/`la`/`lui`/`me`/`te`…) + **stacking** (`je l'y vois`) — far later (`trap:object-pronoun-stacking-too-early`).
- **multi-verb `y`** (`j'y suis`, `j'y pense`) and **broad `à`-replacement** (any `à + X` → `y`) — `y` stays place + *aller* here (`trap:full-pronoun-system-overload`).
- **`y` + `est-ce que`** production (`est-ce que tu y vas ?`) — recognition only.
- **`y` + pouvoir / futur** (`je peux y aller`, `je vais y aller`) — deferred.
- **full pronoun placement as a rule** — word-order learned **in-expression**, not generalized.
- **location prepositions** (`dans`/`sur`/`sous`…) — unchanged from L8.
- **futur proche** (`je vais + inf.`) — unchanged (recognition from L7).

## 5. Sentence Family (place / movement scene; known engines only)

> Scene: continuing the after-class/movement context — *"You've said where you're going; now say it the natural short way."* Statement → `y` transformation is the spine.

| Role | Sentence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Where-question (recycled)** | `Tu vas où ?` | L8 |
| **Movement statement (recycled)** | `Je vais à la maison.` | L7 — the antecedent |
| **Wrap → `y` (anchor)** | `J'y vais.` | **new core**: `y` replaces `à la maison`, sits before the verb |
| **Suggest / let's go** | `On y va.` | **new core**: `on` + `y` + `va` |
| **Ask (supported)** | `Tu y vas ?` | `tu` + `y` + `vas` |
| **Decline (supported/optional)** | `Je n'y vais pas.` | `ne…pas` (L3) wraps `y vais` |
| **Recover (recycled)** | `Je ne comprends pas. Vous pouvez répéter ?` | L1/L11 repair |

- **Anchor:** `J'y vais.` · **Variations:** `On y va.`, `Tu y vas ?` · **Contrast:** `Je vais à la maison.` (full, L7) vs `J'y vais.` (`y`, L14) vs *`Je vais y.`* (**wrong word-order — trap**) and vs `J'y suis.` (`y` + être — **recognition only**).
- **Interchangeable pieces:** the **subject** toggles `je` / `on` / `tu` (`j'y vais` / `on y va` / `tu y vas`) — a **closed set with aller**; the antecedent is always a **recycled** `à + place`.
- **Forbidden / not-yet-ready substitutions:** no `en` (`j'en ai`); no `il y a` as the same `y`; no `j'y suis`/`j'y pense` production (multi-verb); no `je peux y aller` / `je vais y aller`; no `est-ce que tu y vas ?` production; no object pronouns/stacking; no `je vais y` (word-order); no location prepositions; no past/future.

## 9. Natural Reveal / Feedback

**Place-`y` (signature):**
- **Expected:** `J'y vais.` / `On y va.`
- **Acceptable alternatives:** the full form `Je vais à la maison.` (L7) stays valid — accept; `j'y vais` is the natural short way.

**Common mistakes (watch list):**
- **Word-order errors** — `je vais y` / `on va y` (English order) and `je y vais` (no elision) — hint: "`y` goes **before** the verb, and `je` → `j'` in front of it: `j'**y** vais`." (`trap:je-vais-y-word-order`)
- **`j'en ai` / `j'en vais`** (`en` instead of `y`) — `en` is a different pronoun, later; here it's `y` for a place. (`trap:y-en-confusion`)
- **`il y a` confusion** (using `il y a` to mean "I go there") — `il y a` = "there is", a different structure, later. (`trap:il-y-a-too-early`)
- **`j'y suis` / `j'y pense` / using `y` with every place phrase** (extending `y` to other verbs or as a broad replacement rule) — recognition only; here `y` rides *aller* in fixed chunks. (`trap:full-pronoun-system-overload`)
- **object-pronoun stacking** (`je l'y vois`) — far later. (`trap:object-pronoun-stacking-too-early`)
- **`je peux y aller` / `est-ce que tu y vas ?`** as production — recognition only; redirect, don't teach. (`trap:je-peux-y-aller-too-early`)

> Feedback stays passive-mirror; no scores/reward language. Recognition forms (`j'y suis`, `en`, `il y a`, `je peux y aller`) are **redirected, never taught on the spot** (contract §11). **Recognition precedes production** — if a learner can't yet produce `y`, mirror the full `je vais à la maison` (owned) without penalty.

## 10. AI Generation Compatibility

> Binds to `docs/syllabus/ai-generation-contract-v1.md`. L14 runs `model-answer-only` (no live AI), so this governs the *next* stage.

- **AI may generate ONLY place-`y` over `aller`** — `j'y vais` / `on y va` / `tu y vas ?` / (supported) `je n'y vais pas` — where the antecedent is an **owned `à + place`**.

## 12. QA / Pilot Findings

- **Is a compact spec enough?** **Yes.** One pronoun, one sense, two-three owned expressions over an owned antecedent; no new vocabulary/tense/verb. The compact form (recognition-weighted, light tables) fits; a full treatment risks padding it into the general pronoun-system lesson. Promote to full only if smoke shows the word-order/replacement needs more scaffolding.
- **Is `y`-light too early / too abstract?** **Borderline, but safe as scoped.** `y` is the first abstract pronoun (word-order shift), but **chunk-first + recognition-first** keeps it concrete: `j'y vais`/`on y va` are owned as expressions, and the antecedent (`à + place`) is owned (L7). The productive system — the genuinely abstract part — is deferred.
- **Does it preserve paid-depth value?** **Yes** — `en`, the object-pronoun system + stacking, and the productive replacement rule stay reserved; L14 spends only the `j'y vais`/`on y va` slice (pronoun analogue of L11/L12 slices).
- **Does it feel test-like?** **No** — short movement scene + a capability close ("say 'let's go' the French way"), passive mirror. The recognition weighting (Notice/Why-This-Works) keeps it exploratory, not a quiz.
- **Should L15 be Devoir/Falloir-light, as the band map says?** **Yes** — L14 (`y`) + L15 (devoir) are 2 consecutive new-engine lessons → **L16 integration** follows (rhythm rule). L14 does not pre-empt L15.
- **Top risks:** (1) **pronoun-system over-opening** (`en`/object pronouns/stacking) off `y`; (2) **word-order** `je vais y`; (3) **`il y a` conflation**; (4) **production-before-recognition** (forcing `y` before it's been met enough). All guarded by §3/§9/§10 + six traps + recognition-first weighting.
