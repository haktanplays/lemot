# Cairn Start Context

Read this before proposing or making any change to Cairn's product canon.

## Operating rules for agents

1. **The Product Brain is the primary product canon.** [[CAIRN_PRODUCT_BRAIN_v1.0]] governs every product question. Where a lower document contradicts it on a product matter, the Product Brain wins.
2. **Check existing canon before proposing changes.** Read the Product Brain and [[DECISION_REGISTER]] first. A new idea is evaluated against existing canon, not adopted on its own.
3. **The Product Brain must not be silently edited.** Any change is explicit, recorded, and traceable. Canon is never overwritten quietly.
4. **Layered ownership.** Curriculum sequence and pedagogy belong to the Curriculum Bible; lesson and chip authoring to the Content Bible; implementation to the Engineering Bible; identity and expression to the Brand Bible. Do not write those details into the Product Brain, and do not promote them to canon here.
5. **Legacy "Le Mot" files are historical sources, not current canon,** unless a note is explicitly marked current. Cairn is the brand; "Le Mot" is a temporary legacy identifier. Preserve legacy notes as evidence; do not mass-rewrite them, and do not treat them as active direction.
6. **Open Questions are not settled decisions.** Anything in [[OPEN_QUESTIONS]] is unresolved and must not be represented, built on, or cited as decided.
7. **Numbers are planning bands unless marked Canonical.** Lesson counts, vocabulary totals, free-path size, drip intervals, and retrieval ranges orient work; they are not promises or specifications.

## Required form for any proposed canon change

A proposal to change product canon must include all of the following before it can be considered:

- **Current canon** — the exact principle(s) affected, cited by Product Brain section and [[DECISION_REGISTER]] ID.
- **Proposed change** — the new wording or decision.
- **Reason** — why the change is warranted.
- **Consequences** — what else in the product this affects (systems, other principles, non-goals).
- **Migration impact** — effect on existing content, data, code, or learner experience.
- **Founder approval requirement** — an explicit statement that the change is not canon until the founder approves it.

Without all six, the proposal is incomplete and is not applied.
