# Visual Design Canon

## Purpose

Active private visual/product design canon. Historical inventories remain useful, but this note holds the current durable direction.

## Current Visual Direction

- Mountaineer/path/cairn metaphor.
- Distant mountain for aspiration.
- Small visible cairn near the learner/path.
- Clear contrast and readable composition.
- Premium illustration tone.
- Calm learning, not noisy gamification.

## App UI Direction

- Warm, clean cards and readable hierarchy.
- No clutter, childish reward theatre, or Duolingo-style overplay.
- Motion supports learning; it must not slow the lesson.
- Feedback should feel gentle, clear, and actionable.

## Illustration Rules

- Consistent line weight.
- Natural composition.
- Cairn visible but not oversized.
- Human/learner can provide scale.
- Colors may remain vivid; reduce colored area if needed instead of washing everything out.

## Lesson UI Rules

- Chips are clear, touch-friendly, and visibly reusable.
- Reference copy such as "in English" should not feel awkward.
- Labels avoid internal jargon.
- Reveal/confirm hierarchy should be obvious.
- Animations should not obscure, delay, or resize core task elements.

## Known Decisions

- Cairn should be small but visible.
- Mountaineer can sit slightly back/larger so the cairn feels natural.
- Do not solve saturation by simply fading all color; adjust composition/area first.
- Weave label changed toward learner-facing copy rather than internal terminology.

## Historical Source Pointers

- [[Tasarım Envanteri]] and [[LeMot - User Journey]] preserve V4-B source material such as asymmetric breathing, hairline borders, circle-glyph status, and typography notes.
- Treat these as design source inputs; active direction remains this note until a repo-safe visual spec is explicitly promoted.
